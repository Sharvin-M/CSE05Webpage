What are Phishing Attacks?

Phishing attacks are a type of social engineering attack where cybercriminals trick individuals into divulging sensitive information such as passwords, credit card numbers, or social security numbers. The attackers often use emails, text messages, or fake websites that look like legitimate ones to trick the victims.

How to Identify a Phishing Attack?

Phishing attacks can be difficult to identify, but there are some signs that you can look for:

  • The sender's email address is not familiar or looks suspicious
  • The email has a generic greeting instead of your name
  • The email contains urgent or threatening language that tries to force you to act quickly
  • The email contains a suspicious link or attachment
  • The website asks you to enter sensitive information or login credentials

How to Protect Yourself?

Here are some tips to protect yourself from phishing attacks:

  • Never click on suspicious links or download attachments from unknown sources
  • Verify the sender's email address before responding to an email
  • Use two-factor authentication to protect your accounts
  • Update your antivirus software regularly
  • Stay informed about the latest phishing tactics
