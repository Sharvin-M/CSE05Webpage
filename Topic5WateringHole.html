What is a Watering Hole Attack?

A watering hole attack is a type of cyber attack in which the attacker infects a legitimate website that a specific group of individuals are known to visit with malware in order to gain access to their systems or data. The attacker may use social engineering tactics to lure victims to the infected website, such as sending targeted phishing emails or using fake login screens.

How to Protect Yourself

  • Be cautious when visiting unfamiliar websites
  • Use a reputable anti-virus software and keep it up-to-date
  • Avoid clicking on suspicious links or downloading files from untrusted sources
  • Keep your software and operating system up-to-date with the latest security patches
  • Be aware of the latest watering hole attack tactics and stay informed about cybersecurity threats

Examples of Watering Hole Attacks

Here are some examples of common watering hole attacks:

  • Infecting a news website that is frequently visited by a target organization or industry
  • Compromising a popular social media platform to target a specific group of users
  • Using fake login pages to trick victims into providing sensitive information, such as usernames and passwords
  • Injecting malware into legitimate software downloads on popular download sites
