What is Vishing?

Vishing is a type of phishing attack that involves voice calls or VoIP services to trick victims into divulging sensitive information or performing certain actions. Attackers often use social engineering techniques to gain the victim's trust and make the attack more convincing.

How to Protect Yourself

  • Be suspicious of unsolicited calls or messages
  • Verify the caller's identity before sharing any sensitive information
  • Do not provide sensitive information over the phone unless you initiated the call
  • Use a reputable anti-virus software and keep it up-to-date
  • Be aware of the latest vishing tactics and stay informed about cybersecurity threats

Examples of Vishing Attacks

Here are some examples of common vishing attacks:

  • Impersonating a bank representative to trick victims into providing login credentials or account information
  • Posing as a technical support representative to gain access to a victim's computer or network
  • Offering fake prizes or discounts in exchange for personal information
  • Using fear tactics, such as threatening legal action or arrest, to pressure victims into providing sensitive information
